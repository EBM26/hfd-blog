How To Protect Your Family From Dangerous Water Contamination

By Nicole Trelour, ND - April 12, 2018

Dangerous runoff and harsh contaminates like this are making their way into Americans' drinking water systems every day.

You may unknowingly be exposing yourself and your loved ones to toxic, health damaging chemicals every time every time you turn on your tap.

As a result, we are facing a public health crisis like never before... with as MILLIONS of Americans are ingesting poisonous chemicals in their drinking water, their coffee, tea, and even in the food they prepare.

The worst part is that this problem isn't isolated to only one area of the United States. This a problem throughout ALL 50 STATES.

The EPA (Environmental Protection Agency) is supposed to protect you, but despite having the power of government oversight and massive resources, the EPA enforces less than 8.3% of violations of the safe water standards. If you think living in a rural area means you're protected from this health hazard, then I have some very bad news... This health crisis is affecting sprawling cities, endless farmlands, and the coasts. Even the elite in Beverly Hills deal with EXCEEDINGLY DANGEROUS levels of chemicals in their drinking water.

Knowledge is power. It is essential you know what's in your water, so I've compiled years of research about this man-made disaster, and its dire effect on our health.  This guide will help you discover everything you need to know to make the most informed decision about finding the safest, most effective water treatment solution.

WHAT'S THE PROBLEM, ANYWAY?

Right now, there are countless public water systems that MILLIONS of Americans depend on for safe drinking water that are failing, broken, and extremely contaminated.

The contamination comes from many different sources and you're supposed to be notified when your drinking water is no longer safe for consumption. To put it bluntly, the EPA largely ignores these issues over 90% of violations are never corrected.

To make matters worse, the standards have not been updated for over 30 years, so only a small number of harmful contaminants are ACTUALLY BEING MONITORED. For example, drugs that include birth control hormones, blood pressure medicines, and pain killers are consistently found in drinking water, yet they are not even regulated or tested for these contaminants. After all, if you don't know it's there, you don't have to fix it.

EPA regulations aren't even scratching the surface of the thousands of health-destroying and cancer-causing chemicals that have been proven to exist in our drinking water. 

Just because your water looks clean or tastes "normal" does not make it safe. If you and your family want to be safe and healthy, you must have an effective water treatment system

THE QUALITIES OF A GOOD WATER FILTRATION SYSTEM

Based on research and feedback from industry experts, these are the MUST HAVES in an exceptional water filtration system:

REVERSE OSMOSIS
Reverse Osmosis is an extremely effective process in which pressurized water is pushed through a membrane-filter to remove amazingly small and even atom-sized contaminants down to almost undetectable amounts. Previously only found in only expensive under-the-counter filtration systems, this is considered the "gold standard" of water filtration.

MULTIPLE STAGE FILTERING
Multiple stage filtering is the only way you can guarantee the water that you and your family consumes not only tastes great but is also safe to drink. Make sure your unit has a carbon block pre-filter (for taste and simple contaminants) and a VOC carbon block post filter (for complex organic contaminants), as well as the Reverse Osmosis filter mentioned above. When it comes to your health, you never want to rely on just one fail-safe.

CERTIFIED TO NSF STANDARDS
It is critical that the water purifier you purchase is certified to NSF (National Sanitation Foundation) Standards. NSF is a global public health and safety organization and is the authority for water filter standards. The highly-coveted NSF certification guarantees the water filter system is of the highest quality and meets 7 highly stringent and extremely important conditions:

  1. Application and information submission
  2. Product evaluation
  3. Product testing in lab
  4. Manufacturing facility inspection, product confirmation, and product sampling
  5. Test results review and acceptance
  6. Contract signature and product listing
  7. Annual plant inspection and retesting

For maximum protection, the filter system should be tested to NSF standards covering taste (#42), organic contaminants (#53), inorganic contaminants (#58), and emerging contaminants such as drug residue (#401). It's frightening to realize that the best-selling pitcher purifier is certified only under NSF standards #42 and #53 and can remove no more than 6 chemicals.
